Swedish Massage

A gentle, relaxing massage that uses long, smooth strokes, kneading, and circular movements. It's designed to improve circulation, ease muscle tension, and promote relaxation.

×

Deep Tissue Massage

Focuses on the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. It uses slow, firm pressure to target chronic tension, muscle knots, and injuries. This type of massage is ideal for people with persistent pain or stiffness.

×

Sports Massage

Specifically designed for athletes, this massage focuses on preventing and treating injuries related to sports activities. It uses techniques like deep tissue massage and stretching to enhance flexibility, reduce muscle soreness, and speed up recovery.

Hot Stone Massage

Involves the use of smooth, heated stones placed on specific points on the body. The heat helps to relax muscles, improve circulation, and promote relaxation. It's often combined with Swedish massage techniques.

×

Aromatherapy Massage

Combines essential oils with a relaxing massage. The essential oils are chosen for their therapeutic properties, such as stress relief, pain reduction, or mood enhancement. The oils are absorbed through the skin and help to improve mental and physical well-being.

×

Shiatsu Massage

A Japanese technique that uses finger, thumb, and palm pressure to stimulate specific pressure points along the body’s energy pathways. It aims to balance the body's energy, improve circulation, and reduce stress.

×

Thai Massage

A traditional massage that combines stretching, acupressure, and yoga-like movements. The therapist uses their hands, feet, elbows, and knees to apply pressure and guide the client into various poses. It's designed to improve flexibility, relieve tension, and promote overall relaxation.

×

Reflexology

Focuses on pressure points in the feet, hands, and ears, which are believed to correspond to different organs and systems in the body. Reflexology aims to promote healing and relaxation throughout the entire body.

×

Lomi Lomi Massage

A Hawaiian massage technique that uses long, flowing strokes and focuses on the mind-body connection. It incorporates the use of forearms and elbows to create a rhythmic, soothing experience.

×

Cupping Therapy

Involves the use of glass or silicone cups that create suction on the skin. This therapy is believed to help relieve pain, inflammation, and muscle tension by increasing blood flow and loosening tight muscles.

×

Prenatal Massage

A massage designed for pregnant women, focusing on relieving the physical discomforts of pregnancy, such as back pain, swelling, and fatigue. The therapist uses gentle techniques and supports the body with pillows to ensure comfort.

×

Indian Head Massage

Focuses on the head, neck, and shoulders. It involves massaging pressure points to relieve tension and stress. It's often used to alleviate headaches, improve circulation, and promote relaxation.

×

Myofascial Release

A therapeutic technique that targets the fascia, the connective tissue surrounding muscles. By applying gentle, sustained pressure, it helps release tightness and restore movement in the body.

×

Trigger Point Therapy

Targets specific areas of muscle tightness (trigger points) that cause pain and discomfort. The therapist applies direct pressure to these points to release muscle knots and alleviate pain.

×

Chair Massage

A quick, seated massage that targets the back, shoulders, neck, and arms. It's often done in a chair and is great for relieving tension in a short amount of time, typically during work breaks.

×

Tui Na Massage

A form of Chinese therapeutic massage that combines kneading, rolling, and pressing to stimulate the body's energy channels. It’s often used in conjunction with acupuncture to balance the body's energy and relieve pain.

×

Traditional Finnish Sauna

The most common sauna type, using a wood stove or electric heater to heat stones. The heat is dry, with temperatures ranging from 70°C to 100°C (158°F to 212°F). Water can be poured on the stones to create steam (known as löyly).

×

Infrared Sauna

Uses infrared heaters to emit light that is absorbed by the skin, heating the body directly rather than the air. This type operates at lower temperatures (typically between 45°C and 60°C or 113°F to 140°F) and provides a more gentle heat.

×

Steam Sauna (Turkish Bath or Hammam)

In a steam sauna, water is heated to produce steam that fills the room, creating a humid environment. The temperature is usually lower, around 40°C to 50°C (104°F to 122°F), with high humidity (100%). It’s ideal for improving circulation and skin health.

×

Wet Sauna

A mix between the traditional sauna and steam room, it involves both heat and steam. It's designed for high humidity but with dry heat. People often pour water on hot stones to increase humidity levels.

×

Infrared Light Sauna

This sauna uses a combination of infrared light and heat to promote relaxation and healing. It operates at a lower temperature than traditional saunas but provides targeted heat that helps relieve muscle tension and detoxify the body.

×

Salt Sauna

Similar to a traditional sauna, but with salt-lined walls. The heat combined with salt inhalation is said to promote respiratory health and skin rejuvenation.

×

Bio Sauna

A hybrid of the Finnish sauna and steam sauna, the bio sauna uses a lower temperature (50°C to 60°C or 122°F to 140°F) and higher humidity than the Finnish sauna. It's often used for a gentler sauna experience.

×

Russian Banya

A traditional Russian sauna that’s similar to the Finnish sauna but includes the use of a venik (a bundle of twigs) for gentle tapping, which is believed to enhance circulation.

×

Wooden Sauna

Similar to the Finnish sauna, but often designed for outdoor use. It’s constructed entirely of wood, adding an organic aesthetic to the experience.

×

Portable Sauna

Smaller and more affordable, portable saunas use infrared or steam technology and are typically made for personal use at home. They can be set up quickly and packed away after use.
